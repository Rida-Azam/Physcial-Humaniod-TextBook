This chapter provides an overview of the current state and projected future of humanoid robotics from 2025 to 2030. We will explore key players, emerging technologies, ethical considerations, and the potential impact of humanoids across various industries.

## 2.1 Current State of Humanoid Robotics

As of 2025, humanoid robotics is experiencing a resurgence, driven by advancements in AI, sensor technology, and mechanical design. Companies like Boston Dynamics (e.g., Atlas), Figure AI (e.g., Figure 01), and Unitree Robotics (e.g., H1) are demonstrating increasingly capable bipedal systems with improved balance, locomotion, and manipulation abilities.

## 2.2 Emerging Technologies and Trends

Several key technologies are expected to mature and drive humanoid capabilities between 2025 and 2030:

-   **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models**: The integration of large language models with visual perception and robotic control allows humanoids to understand complex commands and adapt to novel situations.
-   **Advanced Actuation**: Development of lighter, more powerful, and energy-efficient actuators will enhance robot agility and endurance.
-   **Tactile Sensing**: High-resolution tactile sensors will improve manipulation dexterity and human-robot interaction safety.
